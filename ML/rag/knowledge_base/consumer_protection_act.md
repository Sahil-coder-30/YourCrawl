# Consumer Protection Act, 2019 (India) & CCPA Dark Pattern Guidelines, 2023

## Part I — Consumer Protection Act, 2019

### Chapter I — Preliminary

#### Section 2 — Definitions

**Consumer**: Any person who buys any goods for consideration or hires or avails of any service for consideration, including e-commerce transactions. Does not include a person who obtains goods for resale or commercial purposes.

**Unfair Trade Practice (Section 2(47))**: A trade practice which, for the purpose of promoting the sale, use, or supply of any goods or for the provision of any service, adopts any unfair method or unfair or deceptive practice. This includes:
- Making any statement, orally or in writing or by visible representation, which falsely represents that goods are of a particular standard, quality, or grade
- Making false or misleading representation concerning the need for or usefulness of any goods or services
- Giving false or misleading facts disparaging the goods, services, or trade of another person
- Selling goods or providing services that do not comply with the standards prescribed
- Making representation that purports to be a warranty or guarantee when it is materially misleading or inadequate
- Permitting the publication of advertisement which materially misleads the consumer

**Unfair Contract (Section 2(46))**: A contract between a manufacturer/trader/service provider and a consumer having terms that cause significant change in the rights of the consumer including:
- Requiring excessive security deposits
- Imposing disproportionate penalty on the consumer for breach
- Refusing to accept early repayment subject only to applicable penalty
- Terminating a contract without reasonable cause
- Transferring a contract to the detriment of the consumer without consent
- Imposing any unreasonable charge, obligation, or condition

**Electronic Commerce (Section 2(16))**: Buying or selling of goods or services, including digital products, over digital or electronic network.

### Chapter III — Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA)

#### Section 18 — Establishment of CCPA
The Central Government shall establish the Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA) to regulate matters relating to violation of consumer rights, unfair trade practices, and false or misleading advertisements.

#### Section 18(2) — Functions of CCPA
- Protecting and enforcing the rights of consumers as a class
- Preventing unfair trade practices
- Ensuring no false or misleading advertisement is made
- Ensuring no person takes part in false or misleading advertisement
- Inquiring or investigating into violations of consumer rights

#### Section 21 — Powers of CCPA for Unfair Trade Practice and Misleading Advertisement
The CCPA may:
- Issue directions to the trader or manufacturer to discontinue unfair trade practice
- File complaint before District, State, or National Commission
- Order recall of goods or withdrawal of services
- Order reimbursement of price paid
- Issue directions for discontinuation of misleading advertisement
- Impose penalty on manufacturer/endorser for false/misleading advertisement

### Chapter IV — Consumer Disputes Redressal

#### Section 34 — Jurisdiction of District Commission
Complaints where value of goods/services does not exceed ₹1 crore.

#### Section 47 — Jurisdiction of State Commission
Complaints where value exceeds ₹1 crore but does not exceed ₹10 crore.

#### Section 58 — Jurisdiction of National Commission
Complaints where value exceeds ₹10 crore.

### Chapter VI — Product Liability

#### Section 82 — Product Liability
A product liability action may be brought by a complainant against a product manufacturer, product service provider, or product seller for any harm caused by a defective product or deficiency in services.

#### Section 84 — Liability of Product Manufacturer
A product manufacturer shall be liable in a product liability action if:
- The product contained a manufacturing defect
- The product was defective in design
- There was a deviation from manufacturing specifications
- The product did not conform to the express warranty
- The product lacked adequate instructions or warnings

### Chapter VII — Penalties

#### Section 88 — Penalties for Misleading Advertisement
- First offence: imprisonment up to 2 years and fine up to ₹10 lakh
- Subsequent offences: imprisonment up to 5 years and fine up to ₹50 lakh
- CCPA may prohibit endorser from making endorsement for up to 1 year (first offence) or 3 years (subsequent)

#### Section 89 — Penalty for Manufacturing, Selling, Storing, Distributing or Importing Adulterants
- If no injury: Imprisonment up to 6 months and fine up to ₹1 lakh
- If injury: Imprisonment up to 1 year and fine up to ₹3 lakh
- Grievous hurt: Imprisonment up to 7 years and fine up to ₹5 lakh
- Death: Not less than 7 years, extendable to imprisonment for life, and fine not less than ₹10 lakh

## Part II — CCPA Guidelines for Prevention and Regulation of Dark Patterns, 2023

### Guideline 1 — Definitions and Scope
**Dark Patterns**: Any practice or deceptive design pattern using UI/UX interactions on any platform that is designed to mislead or trick users to do something they originally did not intend or want to do, by subverting or impairing the consumer autonomy, decision making or choice, amounts to misleading advertisement or unfair trade practice or violation of consumer rights.

This applies to all platforms, including advertisers and sellers, offering goods or services in India, regardless of whether the platform is established in India.

### Guideline 2 — Specified Dark Patterns

The following dark patterns are identified:

### Guideline 3 — False Urgency
Creating a false sense of urgency or scarcity to mislead a user into making an immediate purchase or immediate action. This includes:
- Fake countdown timers showing deal expiry
- Claims of limited stock without genuine basis
- Artificial "last room/item available" messages
- Time-pressure pop-ups ("Buy in next 5 minutes for extra discount")

**Penalty**: Constitutes unfair trade practice. CCPA may impose penalties under Section 21 of CPA 2019.

### Guideline 4 — Basket Sneaking
Adding additional items (products, services, donations) to the shopping cart at checkout without the user's consent, increasing the total amount payable. This includes:
- Pre-selected add-on insurance
- Charity donations added without explicit consent
- Extended warranty pre-selected at checkout
- Hidden service charges added in final step

**Penalty**: Constitutes unfair trade practice. Additional items must be opt-in, not opt-out.

### Guideline 5 — Confirm Shaming
Using language, imagery, or emotion to create fear, shame, ridicule, or guilt to nudge the user towards a particular action. This includes:
- "No thanks, I don't care about saving money"
- Making the decline option feel morally inferior
- Using disappointed emojis or guilt-inducing imagery
- "I hate discounts" as a dismiss option

**Penalty**: Constitutes unfair trade practice.

### Guideline 6 — Forced Action
Forcing a user into buying additional goods, subscribing to unrelated services, or signing up for something, in order to access the original intended product/service. This includes:
- Requiring app download to access content available on web
- Forcing social media login to use service
- Mandatory newsletter subscription to complete purchase
- Requiring premium membership for basic features previously free

**Penalty**: Constitutes unfair trade practice.

### Guideline 7 — Subscription Trap
Making cancellation of a subscription impossible or unreasonably difficult while making it easy to subscribe. This includes:
- No visible cancellation option in settings
- Requiring phone call to cancel but allowing one-click subscribe
- Multi-step cancellation with repeated confirmshaming
- Hiding cancellation behind "Contact Support" or email-only process
- Auto-renewal without clear prior notice

**Penalty**: Constitutes unfair trade practice. Critical severity.

### Guideline 8 — Interface Interference
Designing, modifying, or manipulating user interface elements to subvert or impair user choice or autonomy. This includes:
- Making the "Accept" button prominent while hiding "Decline"
- Asymmetric button sizing favouring the platform's preferred option
- Using colour to make the undesired option seem disabled
- Pre-checking consent boxes
- False hierarchy making certain options harder to find

**Penalty**: Constitutes unfair trade practice.

### Guideline 9 — Bait and Switch
Advertising a particular outcome based on the user's action but deceptively serving an alternate outcome. This includes:
- Advertising one price and charging differently
- Showing product A in advertisement but delivering product B
- Free trial that transitions to paid without clear notice
- "Free shipping" with hidden minimum order requirements

**Penalty**: Constitutes misleading advertisement and unfair trade practice.

### Guideline 10 — Drip Pricing
Making the user pay more than the initially shown price by adding extra charges (fees, taxes, charges) through the purchase journey. This includes:
- Platform fees revealed only at checkout
- Service charges added incrementally
- Packaging charges, handling fees added at final step
- "Convenience charges" not shown in listed price

**Penalty**: Constitutes unfair trade practice.

### Guideline 11 — Disguised Advertisement
Presenting advertisements as independent content such as user-generated content, editorial, or independent reviews when they are paid promotions. This includes:
- Sponsored results not clearly labelled
- Paid reviews disguised as organic reviews
- Native ads indistinguishable from editorial content
- Influencer partnerships without disclosure

**Penalty**: Constitutes misleading advertisement under Section 2(28) of CPA 2019.

### Guideline 12 — Nagging
Repeatedly and persistently making the same request which the user has already denied, disrupting the intended use of the platform. This includes:
- Persistent app rating pop-ups after dismissal
- Repeated premium upgrade banners
- Notification permission requests after denial
- Continuous location access pop-ups

**Penalty**: Constitutes unfair trade practice. Medium severity.

### Guideline 13 — Trick Questions
Using confusing language, double negatives, or ambiguous phrasing in consent forms or user interfaces to trick the user into giving an unintended response. This includes:
- "Uncheck this box if you do NOT want to NOT receive emails"
- Confusing toggle labels where ON means opt-out
- Consent text requiring interpretation to understand
- Terms phrased to imply consent to additional processing

**Penalty**: Constitutes unfair trade practice.

### Guideline 14 — SaaS Billing Pattern (Rogue Malwares)
Practices that mislead users about the nature or necessity of software or services through fake alerts, fake system warnings, or deceptive pop-ups to trick users into downloading unnecessary software or paying for unnecessary services.

**Penalty**: Constitutes both unfair trade practice and misleading advertisement.

## Dark Pattern Implications Under Consumer Protection Law

### E-Commerce Specific Provisions
The Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020 require e-commerce entities to:
- Not manipulate prices of goods or services to gain unreasonable profit
- Not discriminate between consumers of the same class
- Provide information about refund, exchange, warranty, and guarantee
- Display total price including all charges before checkout
- Provide clear cancellation and return policies
- Not use false or misleading advertising

### Combined Regulatory Exposure
Websites using dark patterns face combined exposure under:
1. **CPA 2019**: Unfair trade practice proceedings before Consumer Commissions
2. **CCPA Dark Pattern Guidelines 2023**: Administrative penalties and cease-and-desist orders
3. **DPDP Act 2023**: Data protection violations for consent-related dark patterns
4. **IT Act 2000**: Provisions against deceptive computing practices
5. **Advertising Standards Council of India (ASCI)**: Self-regulatory advertising code violations

### Enforcement Actions
- CCPA can issue cease-and-desist orders
- Consumer Commissions can award compensation and punitive damages
- Class action lawsuits possible under CPA 2019
- Penalty for repeat offenders significantly increases
- International platforms operating in India are subject to Indian consumer protection law
